Just a small designer vs the world


When I started my studies in system engineering, I decided I wanted to start putting my personal time career into web design. After a couple of weeks I was able to find a small book about html and css while also taking local classes with a profesional PHP web developer. I built my first site, straight from PSD to HTML and I was proud of it. Now it's two years later and I've come a long way from building my site from sliced up tables.

Just like my experince and skillsets, the world has also evolved. Every year thousands of companies are competing for not only a physical marketplace, but also a digital one too. Some people believe that space is the final frontier, but I'm pretty sure the internet has that one beat. Of course, the question now is, what foothold do you have on this frontier?


I like to see websites as the modern day equivalent to business cards. They show what you have to offer, can be easily pass around to potential clients but they offer something more. They offer a direct window to that person or business, in a the form of an email or contact fourm. They can actually move your products by going to a store page, or maybe pay for services with certain API integrations like PayPal, AmazonPayments and other services

If you are looking for a simple landing page redesign, or a complete overhaul, I've got you covered. With my two years of coding I've been bashing my head on learning how to get design trends perfect so you don't have to. From mom and pop shops to small business fronts, I'm ready to get coding for you, but don't take my word for it...
